About the NBA

The National Basketball Association (NBA) is the world's premier professional basketball league and one of the most popular sports leagues globally. Founded on June 6, 1946, in New York City as the Basketball Association of America (BAA), it merged with the rival National Basketball League in 1949 and officially became the NBA. Today it stands as a cultural and commercial powerhouse, blending elite athletic competition with entertainment, fashion, music, and global influence.

The league consists of 30 teams: 29 in the United States and one in Canada (the Toronto Raptors). These teams are split evenly into the Eastern Conference and Western Conference, with each conference containing three divisions of five teams. Every team plays an 82-game regular season from mid-October to mid-April, followed by a 16-team playoff tournament that ends with the NBA Finals in June, where the champion is crowned in a best-of-seven series.

The NBA is renowned for its superstar-driven style of play, spectacular athleticism, and global reach.
